Boost Your Curb Appeal and Home Value: The Power of Siding and Gutters

March 28, 2026
5 min read
By Midwestern Construction Team

When homeowners think about improving their property value, they usually think roofing, kitchens, or bathrooms. But two of the highest-impact exterior upgrades — new siding and properly functioning gutters — are consistently overlooked. For Northeast Ohio homeowners, these aren't just cosmetic choices. They're critical layers of protection against a climate that swings from 90°F summers to brutal lake-effect winters, with everything in between.

At Midwestern Construction, we've seen firsthand how siding and gutter upgrades transform a home — both visually and structurally. Here's what you need to know.

More Than a Facelift: The Financial Return of New Siding

New siding consistently ranks among the top home improvement projects for return on investment. But beyond resale value, there are three practical reasons Northeast Ohio homeowners choose to upgrade:

Enhanced Durability

Ohio's freeze-thaw cycle is relentless. Older wood siding absorbs moisture, expands, contracts, and eventually warps, cracks, or rots. Modern vinyl and fiber-cement composites are engineered to handle this cycle without degrading. They don't absorb moisture, they resist impact, and they won't become a meal for insects or mold.

Improved Energy Efficiency

Insulated vinyl siding creates a continuous thermal envelope around your home, dramatically reducing thermal bridging — the heat loss that occurs at studs and seams in older construction. Many homeowners report noticeable reductions in their heating and cooling bills within the first year after installation.

Increased Home Value

Curb appeal directly affects perceived value. A home with fresh, well-maintained siding signals to buyers that the property has been cared for — and that there's less deferred maintenance lurking inside. In competitive markets like Akron and the surrounding Summit County communities, that first impression matters.

"New siding is one of the few home improvements where the financial return is nearly immediate — both in energy savings and in buyer perception at resale."

The Unsung Hero: How Functional Gutters Protect Your Investment

Gutters may be the most underappreciated component of your home's exterior. When they work, you don't think about them. When they fail, the consequences ripple through your entire home:

  • Foundation damage — Water pooling against your foundation from overflowing gutters causes erosion, cracking, and basement flooding. Foundation repair in Northeast Ohio can run into the tens of thousands of dollars.
  • Ice dams — Clogged gutters prevent proper drainage in winter, contributing directly to ice dam formation. (See our ice dam guide for a full breakdown.)
  • Siding and soffit damage — Water that can't flow through gutters cascades down your siding, rotting the soffit and fascia and eventually working its way inside.
  • Landscape erosion — Concentrated water flow from overflows eats away at mulch, soil, and plantings around your home's perimeter.

We recommend seamless aluminum gutters for most Northeast Ohio homes — they have fewer joints, which means fewer failure points and less maintenance than sectional gutters. Gutter guards are worth considering if you have mature trees overhead.

A Unified System: The Synergy of Siding and Gutters

Your home's exterior functions best as a unified system. New siding that drains properly into well-functioning gutters, that carry water away from a sound foundation — each component supports the others. When one fails, the others are put under stress.

When we assess a home for siding, we always inspect the gutter system as part of the same evaluation. Replacing siding while leaving compromised gutters in place is a false economy — the water damage will undo your investment faster than you'd expect.

Conversely, a full exterior upgrade — new roof, new siding, new gutters — done at once is often the smartest financial move. Labor overlaps, materials can be coordinated for color and aesthetic consistency, and you get a cohesive finished look that maximizes both curb appeal and resale value.
