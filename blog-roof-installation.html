Your Roof Installation, Demystified: Our Honest, Step-by-Step Process

March 28, 2026
6 min read
By Midwestern Construction Team

A roof replacement is one of the most significant investments a homeowner can make. Yet for most people, the process feels like a black box — trucks show up, workers hammer away, and a few days later you have a new roof, hopefully. At Midwestern Construction, we believe you deserve better than that. Transparency, integrity, and clear communication aren't just words we put on our website — they're how we run every single job.

Here's exactly what happens when you work with us, from the first call to the final handshake.

Step 1: Inspection & Estimate

Every project begins with a free, no-obligation inspection. We don't just eyeball your roof from the driveway. Our team gets up there — and on complex or large-slope roofs, we deploy drones to capture every angle safely and accurately.

What we're looking for:

  • Missing, cracked, or curling shingles
  • Damaged or improperly installed flashing around chimneys, vents, and skylights
  • Soft spots or sagging sections that may indicate decking damage below
  • Signs of improper ventilation — a leading cause of premature roof failure in Ohio
  • Granule loss in gutters, a sign your shingles are nearing end-of-life

After the inspection, we sit down with you and walk through a detailed, itemized estimate — no vague line items, no hidden fees. We'll also give you our honest recommendation: is a repair sufficient, or does the whole roof need to go? We'll never push a full replacement if it isn't warranted.

"We'll never push a full replacement if a repair will do the job. Our business is built on repeat customers and referrals — and that only happens when homeowners trust us."

Step 2: Material Selection & Planning

Once you decide to move forward, we help you choose the right materials for Ohio's climate. Not all shingles perform the same under Northeast Ohio's freeze-thaw cycles, heavy rain, and occasional hail.

We'll walk you through your options — from standard 3-tab asphalt to impact-resistant architectural shingles rated for hail — and explain the trade-offs in cost, longevity, and warranty coverage. We partner with top-tier manufacturers so every material we use is backed by a strong product warranty on top of our own lifetime craftsmanship warranty.

We'll also coordinate the delivery schedule and talk you through what to expect: where the dumpster goes, how to protect your landscaping, and when the crew will arrive each day. No surprises.

Step 3: Expert Installation

Installation day is where the rubber meets the road — or rather, where the shingles meet the deck. Here's what our crew does:

Preparation

We remove all existing roofing material down to the decking. Every square inch of the deck gets inspected, and any rotted or damaged wood is replaced before we lay a single new layer. Skipping this step is how contractors create problems that show up three years later — we don't skip it.

Protection

Ice and water shield goes down first along the eaves and in valleys — critical in Ohio where ice dams are a real threat every winter. We then apply a synthetic underlayment over the entire deck for a secondary moisture barrier that far outperforms the old felt paper.

Ventilation & Finishing

Proper attic ventilation is installed or verified before shingles go on. A poorly ventilated attic can void your shingle warranty and cut years off the life of your new roof. Ridge vents, soffit vents, and intake/exhaust balance are all checked. Then shingles are installed per the manufacturer's specifications, and all flashing is carefully sealed.

Thorough Cleanup

At the end of each day — and again at the end of the project — we walk the entire property with a rolling magnetic nail sweeper. Your driveway, lawn, and landscaping are left as clean as we found them. Roofing nails in a tire or a child's foot are not acceptable to us.

Step 4: Final Walk-Through & Warranty

Before we pack up, we do a final walk-through with you. We want you to see the finished work, ask any questions, and feel completely satisfied before we consider the job done. We photograph the completed roof for our records and yours.

Every job comes with our lifetime warranty on craftsmanship. If something we installed fails due to our workmanship, we come back and make it right — period. Combined with the manufacturer's product warranty, you're protected on all fronts.
